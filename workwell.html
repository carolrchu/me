workwell

A personal project by yours truly.

Introduction

Two months into my first work term, I met up with one of my closest friends from my program to catch up.

This is what our conversation looked like.

Friend: Hey how has your co-op term been?
Me: It’s been so fun and insightful. It’s crazy how much I look forward to going to work everyday. What about you?
Friend: Oh really? My job is pretty boring, I can’t wait to get back to school when this term is over.

This got me thinking to the beginning of the term, when we were both so excited for our first co-op. Even though we were both in design positions, how come our experiences were so different? What do students actually care about in a job? And what differentiates a good job from a great one? These were some of the questions I asked my colleagues and peers over the past four months. Here’s what they said:

“Working on projects that interest me is most important.”
“I care about the team energy and structure, how much impact will I have as a co-op?”
“Work environment plays a key role. I want to work in an office setting that motivates me to be productive.”
“I care that the company I’m working for shares the same values as me.”

At the end of the day, employee satisfaction and happiness, a lot of the time, boils down to company culture.

Why is this important?

According to a study done by the Washington State University, happy employees are, on average, 31% more productive while unhappy employees are 10% less productive, costing US businesses approximately $450-500 billion each year.

Problem Statement

How might we help students find employment opportunities that fit their interests and needs to not only improve the wellbeing of the company but also the wellbeing of the employee?

Who are we designing for?

Meet Ava. In this case study, we're going to help Ava find companies and job opportunities that suit her preferences.

What does Ava struggle with currently?

Differentiation.

Today's job postings are long, generic, and hard to differentiate. How do we help Ava decide which postions are actually the right fit for her?

Discoverability.

From my research, I found that what students care about is not the company size or following, it’s the projects they’ll be working on, the people they’ll be working with, and the environment they’ll be working in. Therefore, how can we help Ava find opportunities in startup companies and small businesses that have similar traits to the big corporations most of us are aware of?

Organization.

With so many applications and so little time as Ava is still in school; How can we help Ava manage and track which postings she’s interested in and which ones she’s already applied for?

Differentiation

In order to use the app, Ava will have to create an account with workwell. She'll be asked a series of onboarding questions so that the app can get a better sense of what type of workplace she's works best in and populate her feed accordingly. These questions were framed based on what students said to be the most important factors in a job.

Discoverability

Based on her preferences, Ava's feed will populate with images of different workplaces. This allows her to discover smaller companies and small businesses that have a similar culture to the big corporations we're familar with. In addition, she can also find job postings of interest when she visits specific company pages and save them to her profile.

Organization

On her profile, she can view all the workplaces and job postings she's interested in. When she wants to apply for a position, the app will send her an email with the link to the application. After she's applied, she can move the posting from her interested section to her applied section. This makes it a lot easier for Ava to manage and track her applications on the go.

Feedback and Learnings

After testing workwell with a few potential users, the feedback was mainly positive. Users said that they found the experience to be easy, useful, and understandable. Some flows that I've thought about but haven't yet implemented include understanding how notifications will work, how users can more efficiently apply to jobs directly from the app, and doing more testing to see what questions should be included in the onboarding.
